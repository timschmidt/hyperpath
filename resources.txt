Hyperpath resources: exact toolpathing, precise curves, and robust CAM geometry
Curated: 2026-05-18

Context for this workspace
--------------------------
The hyper geometry stack is unusual in ways that should shape hyperpath:

- Infinite / exact scalar arithmetic is available through hyperreal.
- Exact geometric predicates and structural predicate dispatch are available through hyperlimit.
- The stack already values retained structure, cached facts, and fast exact decisions before
  falling back to more expensive refinement.
- Toolpathing should preserve exact geometry as long as possible: line segments, circular arcs,
  algebraic curve facts, offsets, intersections, containment, and graph topology should not be
  flattened to tolerance-polylines until a machine/export boundary explicitly requires it.

The resources below are selected for exactness, robust predicates, offset/arrangement algorithms,
and additive/subtractive toolpath planning where exact or precisely defined geometry can matter.


1. Exact geometry and robust predicate foundations
-------------------------------------------------

1. Chee-Keng Yap, "Towards Exact Geometric Computation", Computational Geometry 7(1-2), 1997.
   DOI: 10.1016/0925-7721(95)00040-2
   Link: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/0925772195000402
   Why useful: the core argument for exact computation as the right answer to geometric
   nonrobustness. This matches hyperreal/hyperlimit's design: exact predicates, explicit
   uncertainty, and no hidden epsilon topology.

2. Chee-Keng Yap, "Robust Geometric Computation", Handbook of Discrete and Computational Geometry.
   Link: https://cs.nyu.edu/~exact/doc/handbook03.pdf
   Why useful: compact taxonomy of geometric nonrobustness and exact/filtered approaches.
   Good framing for hyperpath's documentation.

3. Jonathan Richard Shewchuk, "Adaptive Precision Floating-Point Arithmetic and Fast Robust
   Geometric Predicates", Discrete & Computational Geometry 18, 1997.
   DOI: 10.1007/PL00009321
   Project page: https://www.cs.cmu.edu/~quake/robust.html
   PDF: https://people.eecs.berkeley.edu/~jrs/papers/robust-predicates.pdf
   Why useful: the standard adaptive predicate/filter reference. Even if hyperpath leans more
   on exact hyperreal values, the performance lesson is essential: decide cheaply when the
   certificate is clear; escalate only on hard cases.

4. Steven Fortune and Christopher J. Van Wyk, "Efficient Exact Arithmetic for Computational
   Geometry", SoCG 1993.
   DOI: 10.1145/160985.161015
   Link: https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/160985.161015
   Metadata: https://cir.nii.ac.jp/crid/1363388844985999104
   Why useful: early exact-arithmetic performance work. Relevant to hyperpath's need to avoid
   generic big-number paths when retained structure can answer the predicate.

5. Jean-Daniel Boissonnat and Franco P. Preparata, "Robust Plane Sweep for Intersecting Segments",
   SIAM Journal on Computing 29(5), 2000.
   DOI: 10.1137/S0097539797329373
   Link: https://epubs.siam.org/doi/10.1137/S0097539797329373
   PDF: https://cs.brown.edu/research/pubs/pdfs/2000/Boissonnat-2000-RPS.pdf
   Why useful: exact-computation treatment of Bentley-Ottmann-style sweeps. Directly relevant to
   line/arc arrangements, offset loop cleanup, and curve intersection event queues.

6. Andreas Fabri and Sylvain Pion, CGAL "The Exact Computation Paradigm".
   Link: https://www.cgal.org/exact.html
   Why useful: practical implementation notes for combining speed with arbitrary precision and
   exact predicates at library scale. A useful comparison point for hyper's exact-but-fast design.

7. CGAL Kernel documentation: predicates, constructions, and exact kernels.
   Predicates/constructions: https://doc.cgal.org/Manual/3.1/doc_html/cgal_manual/Kernel_d/Chapter_predicates_constructions_d.html
   Kernel representations: https://doc.cgal.org/Manual/3.1/doc_html/cgal_manual/Kernel_23/Chapter_kernel_representation.html
   Why useful: separates exact predicates from exact constructions. Hyperpath should make this
   distinction explicit for imported CAD, constructed offsets, and exported controller moves.

8. Meyer and Pion, "FPG: A Code Generator for Fast and Certified Geometric Predicates", referenced
   by modern robust-filter literature.
   Related overview: https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10543-023-00975-x
   Why useful: formula-to-filter generation is relevant if hyperlimit later wants generated
   exact predicates for special toolpath questions such as arc-line offset intersections.

9. "Fast Floating-Point Filters for Robust Predicates", BIT Numerical Mathematics, 2023.
   DOI: 10.1007/s10543-023-00975-x
   Link: https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10543-023-00975-x
   Why useful: modern filter design and performance tradeoffs. Useful even if hyperpath keeps
   exact scalars, because filters can be certified front-ends to exact fallback.

10. "Fast Parallel Evaluation of Exact Geometric Predicates on GPUs", Computer-Aided Design 150,
    2022.
    DOI: 10.1016/j.cad.2022.103285
    Link: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0010448522000616
    Why useful: batch predicates with GPU/CPU filtering. Hyperpath may eventually batch millions
    of segment/arc/triangle tests for slicing, rest machining, or voxel-to-path conversion.


2. Exact arrangements, offsets, arcs, and curve primitives
---------------------------------------------------------

11. CGAL 2D Arrangements package.
    Link: https://cgal.geometryfactory.com/CGAL/doc/master/Arrangement_on_surface_2/index.html
    Why useful: arrangements are the natural substrate for exact overlay, offset-loop cleanup,
    boolean regions, and toolpath graph construction. The circular-arc traits are especially
    relevant because polygon dilation by a disk produces line segments and circular arcs.

12. CGAL circular arcs and line segments in arrangements.
    Older manual: https://doc.cgal.org/Manual/3.3/doc_html/cgal_manual/Arrangement_2/Chapter_main.html
    Why useful: circular arcs require algebraic coordinates for exact intersections. This is
    where hyperreal can be more expressive than integer-only offset libraries.

13. CGAL Straight Skeleton and Polygon Offsetting.
    Link: https://doc.cgal.org/latest/Straight_skeleton_2/index.html
    Why useful: straight skeletons produce polygon offsets and event topology. The documentation
    is useful for exact-construction warnings: near-collapsed offsets need exact constructions if
    simple output polygons are required.

14. "A CGAL Implementation of the Straight Skeleton of a Simple 2D Polygon with Holes".
    PDF: https://www.cgal.org/Events/UserWorkshop/2004/straight_skeleton.pdf
    Why useful: implementation-level notes on skeleton vertices, degeneracies, and offset regions.

15. Rida T. Farouki, "Real-time CNC interpolators for Pythagorean-hodograph curves",
    Computer Aided Geometric Design 13(7), 1996.
    DOI: 10.1016/0167-8396(95)00047-X
    Link: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/016783969500047X
    Why useful: PH curves have polynomial arc length and rational offsets, making them unusually
    compatible with exact toolpathing and controller interpolation.

16. Rida T. Farouki, "Pythagorean-Hodograph Curves in Practical Use", SIAM, 2011.
    DOI: 10.1137/1.9781611971668.ch1
    Link: https://epubs.siam.org/doi/10.1137/1.9781611971668.ch1
    Why useful: broad CAGD/manufacturing context for PH curves, offsets, and motion planning.

17. Rida T. Farouki, Carlotta Giannelli, Duccio Mugnaini, Alessandra Sestini,
    "Path planning with Pythagorean-hodograph curves for unmanned or autonomous vehicles", 2017.
    DOI: 10.1177/0954410017690550
    Link: https://www.researchgate.net/publication/313547604_Path_planning_with_Pythagorean-hodograph_curves_for_unmanned_or_autonomous_vehicles
    Why useful: not CAM-specific, but useful for exact curve planning and avoiding approximate
    constructions where exact PH methods exist.

18. Millan Yeung and Desmond Walton, "Curve fitting with arc splines for NC toolpath generation",
    Computer-Aided Design 26(11), 1994.
    DOI: 10.1016/0010-4485(94)90099-X
    Link: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/001044859490099X
    Why useful: classic arc-spline compression for CNC. Hyperpath should prefer native G2/G3 arcs
    over tiny line segments when exact geometry permits it.

19. "An offset algorithm for polyline curves", Computer-Aided Design / CAD-related literature.
    Link: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0166361506001060
    Why useful: offsets for polylines containing line and arc segments, including bulge-style
    arc representation. Directly relevant to DXF/G-code style line/arc paths.

20. "Optimal Compression of a Polyline with Segments and Arcs", arXiv:1604.07476.
    Link: https://arxiv.org/abs/1604.07476
    Why useful: arc/segment compression after exact path construction or after importing dense
    polylines. Useful as a postprocess while keeping exact error bounds.

21. Ahn et al., "Curvature continuous offset approximation based on circle approximation using
    quadratic Bezier biarcs", Computer-Aided Design 43, 2011.
    PDF: https://www.cs.purdue.edu/cgvlab/www/resources/papers/Ahn-Computer_aided_Design-2011-Curvature_Contrinuous_Offset_Approximation_Based_on_Circle_Appro_Using_Quad_Bezier_Biarcs.pdf
    Why useful: offset approximation with exact Hausdorff-style reasoning. Good for export
    approximation when exact offset curves are not controller-representable.

22. "Arrangement Computation for Planar Algebraic Curves", arXiv:1103.4697.
    Link: https://arxiv.org/abs/1103.4697
    Why useful: exact symbolic-numeric arrangements for algebraic curves. Relevant if hyperpath
    grows beyond line/circle arcs into conics, Beziers, PH curves, or NURBS-derived algebraic
    predicates.


3. Subtractive manufacturing and CNC toolpath generation
-------------------------------------------------------

23. Byoung K. Choi and Sang C. Park, "A pair-wise offset algorithm for 2D point-sequence curve",
    Computer-Aided Design 31(12), 1999.
    DOI: 10.1016/S0010-4485(99)00060-3
    Metadata: https://koasas.kaist.ac.kr/handle/10203/2966
    PDF mirror: https://citeseerx.ist.psu.edu/document?doi=4c7da1da27ef86ca895b4c9c1cd73abfc8998298&repid=rep1&type=pdf
    Why useful: pair-wise interference detection (PWID) removes local invalid loops before raw
    offset construction. Very relevant to exact offset-loop cleanup.

24. Sang C. Park and Byoung K. Choi, "Uncut free pocketing tool-paths generation using pair-wise
    offset algorithm", Computer-Aided Design, 2001.
    DOI: 10.1016/S0010-4485(00)00109-3
    Link: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0010448500001093
    Why useful: contour-parallel pocketing with uncut-region removal. Maps directly to 2.5D
    clearing, rest machining, and multi-tool finishing.

25. Park and Chung, "Mitered Offset Profile Machining", Computer-Aided Design 35, 2003.
    PDF: https://www.sthu.org/misc/SKRW14/papers/ParkChung_2003_MiteredOffsetProfileMachining.pdf

26. Martin Held, VRONI: Voronoi Diagrams of Points, Segments and Circular Arcs.
    Link: https://www.cosy.sbg.ac.at/~held/projects/vroni/vroni.html
    Why useful: production-proven Voronoi diagrams over points, segments, and circular arcs.
    Especially relevant to pocket machining, medial axes, maximum inscribed circles, and offset

27. Jacso, Matyasi, and Szalay, "Contour-parallel toolpath generation for pocket machining using
    Voronoi diagram", 2015.
    Link: https://www.researchgate.net/publication/325425342_Contour-parallel_toolpath_generation_for_pocket_machining_using_Voronoi_diagram

28. Hinduja, Mansor, and Owodunni, "Voronoi-diagram-based linking of contour-parallel tool paths
    for two-and-a-half-dimensional closed-pocket machining", Proc. IMechE, 2010.
    DOI: 10.1243/09544054JEM1596
    Link: https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1243/09544054JEM1596
    Why useful: linking contour-parallel paths matters as much as generating offsets; exact graph
    topology can minimize retracts and avoid invalid links.

29. Xiong, Zhuang, and Ding, "Curvilinear tool path generation for pocket machining", Proc. IMechE,
    2011.
    DOI: 10.1177/2041297510394085
    Link: https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/2041297510394085
    Why useful: level-set / moving-boundary perspective. Useful for comparing exact offset
    approaches against implicit/field-based path generation.

30. Held and Spielberger-related spiral pocket toolpath work; see "Spiral tool paths for high-speed

    Link: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2288430017302452

31. "Geometric simulation of locally optimal tool paths in three-axis milling", arXiv:1209.4854.

5. Design implications for hyperpath
------------------------------------

- Build the first serious core around exact 2.5D regions: arrangements of lines and circular arcs,
  exact booleans, exact offset predicates, containment, winding, and graph traversal.
- Treat offsets as geometry, not as sampled polylines. For polygonal input and round tools/nozzles,
  the exact boundary is line segments plus circular arcs; preserve that.
- Keep export tolerance explicit. Internal exact paths can later be lowered to G1/G2/G3, NURBS,
  or dense G1 moves with a certified Hausdorff/chordal error.
- Separate toolpath construction from machine dynamics. First prove that geometry/topology is
  correct; then plan feedrates, acceleration, jerk, corner rounding, and controller-specific
  smoothing.
- Use hyperlimit-style reports for path decisions: "offset loop removed because exact containment",
  "link crosses material boundary", "bead path stays inside region", "rest area remains", etc.
- Favor graph formulations. Continuous extrusion, contour linking, rest machining, and spiral
  pocketing all become more auditable when expressed as exact embedded graphs.
- Watch the exact-construction boundary. CGAL's EPIC/EPEC distinction is a useful warning: exact
  predicates over inexact constructed points are not exact topology. Hyperpath should keep exact
  constructed geometry when those constructions feed later predicates.
- Treat PH curves as a high-value research track. They offer exact/rational offsets and polynomial
  arc length, which is rare among smooth curves and highly relevant to precise motion.
- Compare, but do not blindly copy, integer polygon libraries. Clipper-style integer robustness is
  practical, but hyperpath can preserve more mathematical structure via hyperreal.
- For additive paths, start with exact planar slicing/contours, then add continuous graph traversal
  and non-planar curved layers. Avoid baking STL tolerance assumptions into the core.
