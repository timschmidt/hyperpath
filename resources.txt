Hyperpath resources: exact toolpathing, precise curves, and robust CAM geometry
Curated: 2026-05-18

Context for this workspace
--------------------------
The hyper geometry stack is unusual in ways that should shape hyperpath:

- Infinite / exact scalar arithmetic is available through hyperreal.
- Exact geometric predicates and structural predicate dispatch are available through hyperlimit.
- The stack already values retained structure, cached facts, and fast exact decisions before
  falling back to more expensive refinement.
- Toolpathing should preserve exact geometry as long as possible: line segments, circular arcs,
  algebraic curve facts, offsets, intersections, containment, and graph topology should not be
  flattened to tolerance-polylines until a machine/export boundary explicitly requires it.

The resources below are selected for exactness, robust predicates, offset/arrangement algorithms,
and additive/subtractive toolpath planning where exact or precisely defined geometry can matter.


1. Exact geometry and robust predicate foundations
-------------------------------------------------

1. Chee-Keng Yap, "Towards Exact Geometric Computation", Computational Geometry 7(1-2), 1997.
   DOI: 10.1016/0925-7721(95)00040-2
   Link: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/0925772195000402
   Why useful: the core argument for exact computation as the right answer to geometric
   nonrobustness. This matches hyperreal/hyperlimit's design: exact predicates, explicit
   uncertainty, and no hidden epsilon topology.

2. Chee-Keng Yap, "Robust Geometric Computation", Handbook of Discrete and Computational Geometry.
   Link: https://cs.nyu.edu/~exact/doc/handbook03.pdf
   Why useful: compact taxonomy of geometric nonrobustness and exact/filtered approaches.
   Good framing for hyperpath's documentation.

3. Jonathan Richard Shewchuk, "Adaptive Precision Floating-Point Arithmetic and Fast Robust
   Geometric Predicates", Discrete & Computational Geometry 18, 1997.
   DOI: 10.1007/PL00009321
   Project page: https://www.cs.cmu.edu/~quake/robust.html
   PDF: https://people.eecs.berkeley.edu/~jrs/papers/robust-predicates.pdf
   Why useful: the standard adaptive predicate/filter reference. Even if hyperpath leans more
   on exact hyperreal values, the performance lesson is essential: decide cheaply when the
   certificate is clear; escalate only on hard cases.

4. Steven Fortune and Christopher J. Van Wyk, "Efficient Exact Arithmetic for Computational
   Geometry", SoCG 1993.
   DOI: 10.1145/160985.161015
   Link: https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/160985.161015
   Metadata: https://cir.nii.ac.jp/crid/1363388844985999104
   Why useful: early exact-arithmetic performance work. Relevant to hyperpath's need to avoid
   generic big-number paths when retained structure can answer the predicate.

5. Jean-Daniel Boissonnat and Franco P. Preparata, "Robust Plane Sweep for Intersecting Segments",
   SIAM Journal on Computing 29(5), 2000.
   DOI: 10.1137/S0097539797329373
   Link: https://epubs.siam.org/doi/10.1137/S0097539797329373
   PDF: https://cs.brown.edu/research/pubs/pdfs/2000/Boissonnat-2000-RPS.pdf
   Why useful: exact-computation treatment of Bentley-Ottmann-style sweeps. Directly relevant to
   line/arc arrangements, offset loop cleanup, and curve intersection event queues.

6. Andreas Fabri and Sylvain Pion, CGAL "The Exact Computation Paradigm".
   Link: https://www.cgal.org/exact.html
   Why useful: practical implementation notes for combining speed with arbitrary precision and
   exact predicates at library scale. A useful comparison point for hyper's exact-but-fast design.

7. CGAL Kernel documentation: predicates, constructions, and exact kernels.
   Predicates/constructions: https://doc.cgal.org/Manual/3.1/doc_html/cgal_manual/Kernel_d/Chapter_predicates_constructions_d.html
   Kernel representations: https://doc.cgal.org/Manual/3.1/doc_html/cgal_manual/Kernel_23/Chapter_kernel_representation.html
   Why useful: separates exact predicates from exact constructions. Hyperpath should make this
   distinction explicit for imported CAD, constructed offsets, and exported controller moves.

8. Meyer and Pion, "FPG: A Code Generator for Fast and Certified Geometric Predicates", referenced
   by modern robust-filter literature.
   Related overview: https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10543-023-00975-x
   Why useful: formula-to-filter generation is relevant if hyperlimit later wants generated
   exact predicates for special toolpath questions such as arc-line offset intersections.

9. "Fast Floating-Point Filters for Robust Predicates", BIT Numerical Mathematics, 2023.
   DOI: 10.1007/s10543-023-00975-x
   Link: https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10543-023-00975-x
   Why useful: modern filter design and performance tradeoffs. Useful even if hyperpath keeps
   exact scalars, because filters can be certified front-ends to exact fallback.

10. "Fast Parallel Evaluation of Exact Geometric Predicates on GPUs", Computer-Aided Design 150,
    2022.
    DOI: 10.1016/j.cad.2022.103285
    Link: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0010448522000616
    Why useful: batch predicates with GPU/CPU filtering. Hyperpath may eventually batch millions
    of segment/arc/triangle tests for slicing, rest machining, or voxel-to-path conversion.


2. Exact arrangements, offsets, arcs, and curve primitives
---------------------------------------------------------

11. CGAL 2D Arrangements package.
    Link: https://cgal.geometryfactory.com/CGAL/doc/master/Arrangement_on_surface_2/index.html
    Why useful: arrangements are the natural substrate for exact overlay, offset-loop cleanup,
    boolean regions, and toolpath graph construction. The circular-arc traits are especially
    relevant because polygon dilation by a disk produces line segments and circular arcs.

12. CGAL circular arcs and line segments in arrangements.
    Older manual: https://doc.cgal.org/Manual/3.3/doc_html/cgal_manual/Arrangement_2/Chapter_main.html
    Why useful: circular arcs require algebraic coordinates for exact intersections. This is
    where hyperreal can be more expressive than integer-only offset libraries.

13. CGAL Straight Skeleton and Polygon Offsetting.
    Link: https://doc.cgal.org/latest/Straight_skeleton_2/index.html
    Why useful: straight skeletons produce polygon offsets and event topology. The documentation
    is useful for exact-construction warnings: near-collapsed offsets need exact constructions if
    simple output polygons are required.

14. "A CGAL Implementation of the Straight Skeleton of a Simple 2D Polygon with Holes".
    PDF: https://www.cgal.org/Events/UserWorkshop/2004/straight_skeleton.pdf
    Why useful: implementation-level notes on skeleton vertices, degeneracies, and offset regions.

15. Rida T. Farouki, "Real-time CNC interpolators for Pythagorean-hodograph curves",
    Computer Aided Geometric Design 13(7), 1996.
    DOI: 10.1016/0167-8396(95)00047-X
    Link: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/016783969500047X
    Why useful: PH curves have polynomial arc length and rational offsets, making them unusually
    compatible with exact toolpathing and controller interpolation.

16. Rida T. Farouki, "Pythagorean-Hodograph Curves in Practical Use", SIAM, 2011.
    DOI: 10.1137/1.9781611971668.ch1
    Link: https://epubs.siam.org/doi/10.1137/1.9781611971668.ch1
    Why useful: broad CAGD/manufacturing context for PH curves, offsets, and motion planning.

17. Rida T. Farouki, Carlotta Giannelli, Duccio Mugnaini, Alessandra Sestini,
    "Path planning with Pythagorean-hodograph curves for unmanned or autonomous vehicles", 2017.
    DOI: 10.1177/0954410017690550
    Link: https://www.researchgate.net/publication/313547604_Path_planning_with_Pythagorean-hodograph_curves_for_unmanned_or_autonomous_vehicles
    Why useful: not CAM-specific, but useful for exact curve planning and avoiding approximate
    constructions where exact PH methods exist.

18. Millan Yeung and Desmond Walton, "Curve fitting with arc splines for NC toolpath generation",
    Computer-Aided Design 26(11), 1994.
    DOI: 10.1016/0010-4485(94)90099-X
    Link: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/001044859490099X
    Why useful: classic arc-spline compression for CNC. Hyperpath should prefer native G2/G3 arcs
    over tiny line segments when exact geometry permits it.

19. "An offset algorithm for polyline curves", Computer-Aided Design / CAD-related literature.
    Link: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0166361506001060
    Why useful: offsets for polylines containing line and arc segments, including bulge-style
    arc representation. Directly relevant to DXF/G-code style line/arc paths.

20. "Optimal Compression of a Polyline with Segments and Arcs", arXiv:1604.07476.
    Link: https://arxiv.org/abs/1604.07476
    Why useful: arc/segment compression after exact path construction or after importing dense
    polylines. Useful as a postprocess while keeping exact error bounds.

21. Ahn et al., "Curvature continuous offset approximation based on circle approximation using
    quadratic Bezier biarcs", Computer-Aided Design 43, 2011.
    PDF: https://www.cs.purdue.edu/cgvlab/www/resources/papers/Ahn-Computer_aided_Design-2011-Curvature_Contrinuous_Offset_Approximation_Based_on_Circle_Appro_Using_Quad_Bezier_Biarcs.pdf
    Why useful: offset approximation with exact Hausdorff-style reasoning. Good for export
    approximation when exact offset curves are not controller-representable.

22. "Arrangement Computation for Planar Algebraic Curves", arXiv:1103.4697.
    Link: https://arxiv.org/abs/1103.4697
    Why useful: exact symbolic-numeric arrangements for algebraic curves. Relevant if hyperpath
    grows beyond line/circle arcs into conics, Beziers, PH curves, or NURBS-derived algebraic
    predicates.


3. Subtractive manufacturing and CNC toolpath generation
-------------------------------------------------------

23. Byoung K. Choi and Sang C. Park, "A pair-wise offset algorithm for 2D point-sequence curve",
    Computer-Aided Design 31(12), 1999.
    DOI: 10.1016/S0010-4485(99)00060-3
    Metadata: https://koasas.kaist.ac.kr/handle/10203/2966
    PDF mirror: https://citeseerx.ist.psu.edu/document?doi=4c7da1da27ef86ca895b4c9c1cd73abfc8998298&repid=rep1&type=pdf
    Why useful: pair-wise interference detection (PWID) removes local invalid loops before raw
    offset construction. Very relevant to exact offset-loop cleanup.

24. Sang C. Park and Byoung K. Choi, "Uncut free pocketing tool-paths generation using pair-wise
    offset algorithm", Computer-Aided Design, 2001.
    DOI: 10.1016/S0010-4485(00)00109-3
    Link: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0010448500001093
    Why useful: contour-parallel pocketing with uncut-region removal. Maps directly to 2.5D
    clearing, rest machining, and multi-tool finishing.

25. Park and Chung, "Mitered Offset Profile Machining", Computer-Aided Design 35, 2003.
    PDF: https://www.sthu.org/misc/SKRW14/papers/ParkChung_2003_MiteredOffsetProfileMachining.pdf
    Why useful: mitered offsets are important when the desired output is not round-offset geometry
    but controller-friendly, sharp-corner-preserving profile motion.

26. Martin Held, VRONI: Voronoi Diagrams of Points, Segments and Circular Arcs.
    Link: https://www.cosy.sbg.ac.at/~held/projects/vroni/vroni.html
    Why useful: production-proven Voronoi diagrams over points, segments, and circular arcs.
    Especially relevant to pocket machining, medial axes, maximum inscribed circles, and offset
    curves from mixed line/arc boundaries.

27. Jacso, Matyasi, and Szalay, "Contour-parallel toolpath generation for pocket machining using
    Voronoi diagram", 2015.
    Link: https://www.researchgate.net/publication/325425342_Contour-parallel_toolpath_generation_for_pocket_machining_using_Voronoi_diagram
    Why useful: Voronoi/wavefront approach for pockets with circular and linear boundaries.

28. Hinduja, Mansor, and Owodunni, "Voronoi-diagram-based linking of contour-parallel tool paths
    for two-and-a-half-dimensional closed-pocket machining", Proc. IMechE, 2010.
    DOI: 10.1243/09544054JEM1596
    Link: https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1243/09544054JEM1596
    Why useful: linking contour-parallel paths matters as much as generating offsets; exact graph
    topology can minimize retracts and avoid invalid links.

29. Xiong, Zhuang, and Ding, "Curvilinear tool path generation for pocket machining", Proc. IMechE,
    2011.
    DOI: 10.1177/2041297510394085
    Link: https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/2041297510394085
    Why useful: level-set / moving-boundary perspective. Useful for comparing exact offset
    approaches against implicit/field-based path generation.

30. Held and Spielberger-related spiral pocket toolpath work; see "Spiral tool paths for high-speed
    machining of 2D pockets with or without islands".
    Link: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2288430017302452
    Why useful: spiral paths reduce retracts and improve high-speed machining behavior. Good target
    for exact medial-axis or Voronoi-based construction.

31. "Geometric simulation of locally optimal tool paths in three-axis milling", arXiv:1209.4854.
    Link: https://arxiv.org/abs/1209.4854
    Why useful: 3-axis surface milling and local offsets/collision detection over triangulated
    geometry. Relevant to future hypermesh/hyperpath integration.

32. "Brief on tool path generation/optimization methods for multi-axis CNC machining",
    arXiv:2212.07941.
    Link: https://arxiv.org/abs/2212.07941
    Why useful: survey entry point for multi-axis categories: tool orientation, end milling,
    feedrate interpolation, and path optimization.

33. "A survey of path planning and feedrate interpolation in computer numerical control",
    arXiv:2303.01368.
    Link: https://arxiv.org/abs/2303.01368
    Why useful: overview for CNC interpolation and feed scheduling, including NURBS and smoothing.

34. NURBS interpolation with smooth feedrate scheduling.
    DOI: 10.1016/j.ijmachtools.2014.07.002
    Link: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0890695514000960
    Why useful: if hyperpath emits NURBS or spline-compatible controller paths, feed scheduling
    must be considered alongside geometric accuracy.

35. OpenCAMLib.
    PyPI: https://pypi.org/project/opencamlib/
    Docs PDF: https://opencamlib-test.readthedocs.io/_/downloads/en/fix-readthedocs/pdf/
    Why useful: practical open-source reference for drop-cutter, push-cutter, and waterline
    toolpaths. Not exact-first, but valuable for algorithm inventory and CNC concepts.

36. Clipper2.
    Overview: https://www.angusj.com/clipper2/Docs/Overview.htm
    Offsetting: https://www.angusj.com/clipper2/Docs/Units/Clipper.Offset/Classes/ClipperOffset/_Body.htm
    Robustness: https://angusj.com/clipper2/Docs/Robustness.htm
    Why useful: integer-robust polygon clipping/offsetting baseline. Hyperpath can learn from
    its fill rules, offset joins, and integer robustness while avoiding loss of exact arc/algebraic
    structure.

37. CavalierContours.
    Parallel offset notes: https://deepwiki.com/jbuckmccready/cavalier_contours/2.1.4-parallel-offset
    Why useful: practical line-and-arc polyline offsets. A useful implementation comparison for
    preserving circular arcs rather than flattening to polylines.


4. Additive manufacturing slicing and path planning
--------------------------------------------------

38. Andre Dolenc and Ismo Makela, "Slicing procedures for layered manufacturing techniques",
    Computer-Aided Design 26(2), 1994.
    DOI: 10.1016/0010-4485(94)90032-9
    Link: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/0010448594900329
    Why useful: foundational slicing paper. Good baseline for where tolerance-based STL slicing
    started and what exact CAD-native slicing can improve.

39. Tata, Fadel, Bagchi, and Aziz, "Efficient slicing for layered manufacturing",
    Rapid Prototyping Journal 4(4), 1998.
    DOI: 10.1108/13552549810239003
    Link: https://www.researchgate.net/publication/242341408_Efficient_slicing_for_layered_manufacturing
    Why useful: adaptive slicing criteria such as cusp height, chord length, and volumetric error.
    Hyperpath can express these as exact predicates plus explicit export tolerances.

40. "An adaptive slicing and selective hatching strategy for layered manufacturing",
    Journal of Materials Processing Technology, 1999.
    DOI: 10.1016/S0924-0136(99)00043-6
    Link: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0924013699000436
    Why useful: direct slicing of NURBS CAD surfaces, avoiding STL where possible. Strong fit for
    a precisely-defined-geometry stack.

41. "Path Planning Strategies to Optimize Accuracy, Quality, Build Time and Material Use in
    Additive Manufacturing: A Review", Micromachines 2020.
    DOI: 10.3390/mi11070633
    Link: https://www.mdpi.com/2072-666X/11/7/633
    Why useful: broad additive path planning survey: contours, rasters, spirals, continuous paths,
    support, material use, and multi-axis directions.

42. "Continuous Toolpath Planning in Additive Manufacturing", arXiv:1908.07452, and the journal
    version "Continuous toolpath planning in a graphical framework for sparse infill additive
    manufacturing", Computer-Aided Design 127, 2020.
    arXiv: https://arxiv.org/abs/1908.07452
    DOI: 10.1016/j.cad.2020.102880
    Link: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0010448520300737
    Why useful: models infill as a graph and guarantees continuous paths via Eulerian structure.
    This is highly compatible with exact arrangement graphs and topological certificates.

43. "Toolpath Planning for Continuous Extrusion Additive Manufacturing".
    PDF: https://research.engr.oregonstate.edu/rdml/sites/research.engr.oregonstate.edu.rdml/files/toolpath-planning-continuous-final.pdf
    Why useful: formal graph definition of additive toolpaths and continuous extrusion constraints.

44. Roschli et al., "Creating Toolpaths Without Starts and Stops for Extrusion-Based Systems",
    Solid Freeform Fabrication Proceedings, 2019.
    Link: https://www.ornl.gov/publication/creating-toolpaths-without-starts-and-stops-extrusion-based-systems
    Why useful: large-format additive manufacturing emphasizes avoiding starts/stops, wipes, and
    non-printing travel. Exact path graph construction can make continuity constraints explicit.

45. "A novel approach for adaptive skeleton toolpath generation", ORNL / SFF.
    Link: https://www.ornl.gov/publication/novel-approach-adaptive-skeleton-toolpath-generation
    PDF: https://www.sc.fsu.edu/attachments/article/1733/Cx23_White_Liam.pdf
    Why useful: skeleton/Voronoi-based additive paths for filling voids, relevant to sparse infill,
    bead-width-aware paths, and exact medial-axis experiments.

46. Jensen et al., "Toolpath Strategies for 5DOF and 6DOF Extrusion-Based Additive Manufacturing",
    Applied Sciences 9(19), 2019.
    DOI: 10.3390/app9194168
    Link: https://www.mdpi.com/2076-3417/9/19/4168
    Why useful: non-planar/multi-axis path strategies and orientation control. Good bridge between
    additive and subtractive surface-following toolpath concepts.

47. "Five-axis additive manufacturing of freeform models through buildup of transition layers",
    Journal of Manufacturing Systems 50, 2019.
    DOI: 10.1016/j.jmsy.2018.12.002
    Link: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0278612518301419
    Why useful: curved/freeform layers for surface quality and support reduction.

48. "Curved layer based process planning for multi-axis volume printing of freeform models",
    Computer-Aided Design 114, 2019.
    PDF: https://gershon.cs.technion.ac.il/Seminar23_3DP/Papers/NonLayered/AMCurvedLayersMultiAxis2019Kai.pdf
    Why useful: curved-layer slicing, volume printing, and multi-axis planning from exact CAD
    geometry rather than just planar STL layers.

49. "4 and 5-Axis additive manufacturing of parts represented using free-form 3D curves",
    Graphical Models 120, 2022.
    DOI: 10.1016/j.gmod.2022.101137
    Link: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S1524070322000145
    Why useful: free-form 3D curves as input paths, directly relevant to a curve-native hyperpath.

50. "Non-planar 5-axis directional additive manufacturing of plastics: Machine, process, and
    tool path", Additive Manufacturing / Elsevier, 2024.
    Link: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2213846324001822
    Repository page: https://research.sabanciuniv.edu/id/eprint/49974/
    Why useful: modern non-planar directional path generation and bead-direction constraints.

51. "As-Continuous-As-Possible Extrusion Fabrication of Surface Models", arXiv:2201.02374.
    Link: https://arxiv.org/abs/2201.02374
    Why useful: surface decomposition into patches printable with as few continuous paths as
    possible. Useful for shell/surface additive path planning.

52. "Implicit Toolpath Generation for Functionally Graded Additive Manufacturing via
    Gradient-Aware Slicing", arXiv:2505.08093.
    Link: https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.08093
    Why useful: recent implicit/slicing work for graded material fields. Less mature, but relevant
    if hyperpath later supports scalar fields and variable bead/material deposition.


5. PCB trace autorouting and EDA routing
----------------------------------------

53. C. Y. Lee, "An Algorithm for Path Connections and Its Applications", IRE Transactions on
    Electronic Computers EC-10(3), 1961.
    DOI: 10.1109/TEC.1961.5219222
    Link: https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/An-Algorithm-for-Path-Connections-and-Its-Applications-Lee/6b9cbd70349aac279cb69ffb6017ee6504a729b9
    Why useful: foundational wave-propagation maze router for wiring diagrams, PCBs, and grid
    routing. It guarantees a shortest path when one exists, but its memory/runtime costs explain
    why practical autorouters layer heuristics and rip-up/reroute on top.

54. Frank Rubin, "The Lee Path Connection Algorithm", IEEE Transactions on Computers C-23(9),
    1974.
    PDF: https://www.itseng.org/research/papers/topics/VLSI_Physical_Design_Automation/Routing/Global_Routing/A-star_Search/%5BRubin-1974%5D-IEEE_T_Comp.pdf
    Why useful: clarifies Lee's path-cost assumptions and path-consistency restrictions. Relevant
    for hyperpath if path costs include width, via count, impedance class, layer preference, and
    length-matching penalties.

55. David W. Hightower, "A solution to line-routing problems on the continuous plane",
    DAC 1969.
    DOI: 10.1145/800260.809014
    Link: https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/800260.809014
    Metadata: https://cir.nii.ac.jp/crid/1360016867310862336
    Why useful: line-search routing on the continuous plane rather than pure grids. This is a
    natural reference for exact geometry because traces, keepouts, and pads are continuous
    shapes before they are discretized for search.

56. F. O. Hadlock, "A shortest path algorithm for grid graphs", Networks 7(4), 1977.
    DOI: 10.1002/net.3230070404
    Link: https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1002/net.3230070404
    Metadata: https://cir.nii.ac.jp/crid/1361418519827483904?lang=en
    Why useful: detour-based shortest-path search for grid graphs, closely related to A*-style
    routing. Good for exact integer/lattice routing kernels and for proving what a heuristic is
    allowed to prune.

57. Jiri Soukup, "Fast Maze Router", DAC 1978.
    Link: https://janders.eecg.utoronto.ca/1387/readings/soukup.pdf
    Metadata: https://dblp.org/rec/conf/dac/Soukup78
    Why useful: combines direct line search with Lee-style expansion around obstacles. A useful
    template for "try retained structural information first, then fall back to expensive search".

58. Marco Tibaldi, "LAYMAT: A Lee-type router for two-layer wiring", Computers in Industry 1(4),
    1980.
    DOI: 10.1016/0166-3615(80)90020-2
    Link: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/0166361580900202
    Why useful: PCB-specific adaptation of Lee/Rubin routing for two-layer boards, emphasizing
    that local shortest paths do not necessarily maximize completion of all nets.

59. Tan Yan, Qiang Ma, and Martin D. F. Wong, "Advances in PCB Routing", IPSJ Transactions on
    System LSI Design Methodology / Information and Media Technologies, 2012.
    DOI: 10.11185/imt.7.535
    Link: https://www.jstage.jst.go.jp/article/imt/7/2/7_535/_article
    PDF: https://www.jstage.jst.go.jp/article/ipsjtsldm/5/0/5_0_14/_pdf/-char/en
    Why useful: concise survey focused on escape routing and length-matching routing, two of the
    hardest PCB-specific constraints beyond ordinary point-to-point routing.

60. Tan Yan, "Algorithmic studies on PCB routing", Ph.D. dissertation, University of Illinois,
    2011.
    Link: https://www.ideals.illinois.edu/items/18457
    Why useful: deeper companion to the survey above: escape routing, length-constrained routing,
    layer assignment, BGA/PGA routing, and network-flow formulations.

61. Tan Yan, Pei-Ci Wu, Qiang Ma, and Martin D. F. Wong, "On the escape routing of differential
    pairs", ICCAD 2010.
    DOI: 10.1109/ICCAD.2010.5654214
    Link: https://experts.illinois.edu/en/publications/on-the-escape-routing-of-differential-pairs/
    Why useful: differential pair routing couples two traces geometrically and electrically.
    Exact path geometry matters for separation, skew, bend symmetry, and paired obstacle
    avoidance.

62. Tai-Hung Li et al., "Escape routing of differential pairs considering length matching",
    ASP-DAC 2012.
    DOI: 10.1109/ASPDAC.2012.6164934
    Link: https://www.researchgate.net/publication/254019877_Escape_routing_of_differential_pairs_considering_length_matching
    Why useful: combines differential-pair escape with length matching using min-cost median
    points and network flow. Relevant to exact constraints over path length and paired traces.

63. Mustafa Ozdal and Martin D. F. Wong, "A Length-Matching Routing Algorithm for
    High-Performance Printed Circuit Boards", IEEE TCAD 25(12), 2006.
    DOI: 10.1109/TCAD.2006.882584
    Link: https://www.researchgate.net/publication/3225949_A_Length-Matching_Routing_Algorithm_for_High-Performance_Printed_Circuit_Boards
    Why useful: length constraints are where a pure shortest-path router fails. Hyperpath's exact
    curve/path length machinery could make this class of constraint auditable.

64. Tsun-Ming Tseng et al., "Post-Route Alleviation of Dense Meander Segments in
    High-Performance Printed Circuit Boards", ICCAD 2013.
    DOI: 10.1109/ICCAD.2013.6691193
    arXiv: https://arxiv.org/abs/1705.04983
    Why useful: equal physical length is not always equal electrical delay because dense meanders
    couple to themselves. Useful reminder that route geometry must eventually meet signal-
    integrity models, not just topological DRC.

65. Weijie Fang et al., "Obstacle-Aware Length-Matching Routing for Any-Direction Traces in
    Printed Circuit Board", arXiv:2407.19195, 2024.
    DOI: 10.48550/arXiv.2407.19195

6. Design implications for hyperpath
------------------------------------

- Build the first serious core around exact 2.5D regions: arrangements of lines and circular arcs,
  exact booleans, exact offset predicates, containment, winding, and graph traversal.
- Treat offsets as geometry, not as sampled polylines. For polygonal input and round tools/nozzles,
  the exact boundary is line segments plus circular arcs; preserve that.
- Keep export tolerance explicit. Internal exact paths can later be lowered to G1/G2/G3, NURBS,
  or dense G1 moves with a certified Hausdorff/chordal error.
- Separate toolpath construction from machine dynamics. First prove that geometry/topology is
  correct; then plan feedrates, acceleration, jerk, corner rounding, and controller-specific
  smoothing.
- Use hyperlimit-style reports for path decisions: "offset loop removed because exact containment",
  "link crosses material boundary", "bead path stays inside region", "rest area remains", etc.
- Favor graph formulations. Continuous extrusion, contour linking, rest machining, and spiral
  pocketing all become more auditable when expressed as exact embedded graphs.
- Watch the exact-construction boundary. CGAL's EPIC/EPEC distinction is a useful warning: exact
  predicates over inexact constructed points are not exact topology. Hyperpath should keep exact
  constructed geometry when those constructions feed later predicates.
- Treat PH curves as a high-value research track. They offer exact/rational offsets and polynomial
  arc length, which is rare among smooth curves and highly relevant to precise motion.
- Compare, but do not blindly copy, integer polygon libraries. Clipper-style integer robustness is
  practical, but hyperpath can preserve more mathematical structure via hyperreal.
- For additive paths, start with exact planar slicing/contours, then add continuous graph traversal
  and non-planar curved layers. Avoid baking STL tolerance assumptions into the core.
- For PCB routing, separate candidate generation from verification. Exact clearance, keepout,
  pad entry, annular-ring, copper-shape, and layer-transition predicates can certify or reject
  routes proposed by maze routers, flow/SAT solvers, or learned policies.
- Treat trace geometry as first-class geometry. A routed trace is a swept shape with width and
  layer, not just a centerline; exact Minkowski/offset predicates are directly applicable.
- Preserve board constraints as structured facts: net class, width/spacing, via technology,
  impedance class, differential-pair coupling, length bounds, allowed layers, and manually fixed
  routes should all participate in routing decisions.
- Consider DSN/SES import/export early. It gives hyperpath a practical path into KiCad/FreeRouting
  workflows while the internal exact geometry model matures.
